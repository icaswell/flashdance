签订
签订 (qiāndìng) is a formal word, often used in legal or official contexts, for signing agreements, contracts, or treaties.  While it can sometimes be translated simply as "sign," it carries a stronger connotation of formal agreement and commitment.  More casual alternatives for signing something might be 签 (qiān) or 签字 (qiān zì).

事件
事件 (shìjiàn) refers to an incident, event, or happening, often with a negative or noteworthy connotation.  It can imply something unexpected or of public interest.  For more mundane or everyday occurrences, you might use 事情 (shìqing).

这
这 (zhè) is a versatile word with a few key uses.  It functions as a demonstrative pronoun ("this," "these"), a determiner modifying nouns, and a pronoun in certain contexts. Pay attention to how it combines with measure words and nouns. For example,  这只猫 (zhè zhī māo) "this cat."

歌唱
歌唱 (gēchàng) is a more formal and literary word for singing than 唱歌 (chànggē).  It's often used in contexts like discussing musical performances, artistic expression, or praising someone's vocal abilities.

总量
总量 (zǒngliàng) specifically refers to the total quantity or amount of something.  While 总数 (zǒngshù) also means "total," it is generally used for countable nouns, whereas 总量 is for uncountable quantities or mass nouns.

~
亲人
亲人 (qīnrén) refers to close relatives, often implying a strong emotional bond.  It's more intimate than 家人 (jiārén), which means family members and can include more distant relations.

~
读者
读者 (dúzhě) means "reader," typically referring to someone who reads books, magazines, or other written material.  It can refer to a specific reader or a general readership.

~
图书
图书 (túshū) is a more formal and comprehensive term for books and written materials than 书 (shū).  It's often used in contexts like libraries, publishing, or academic discussions.

贴纸
There's no significant difference between 贴纸 (tiēzhǐ) and "sticker" in English usage.

地形
地形 (dìxíng) refers to the physical features and shape of the land surface, encompassing both natural formations and man-made alterations.

~
水产品
水产品 (shuǐchǎnpǐn) refers to a broad category of products harvested from bodies of water, including fish, shellfish, seaweed, and other aquatic organisms.

以上
以上 (yǐshàng) can mean "above," "over," or "more than" when referring to quantities or levels.  It can also mean "the above-mentioned" when summarizing previous points.  Finally, it can signal the conclusion of a speech or statement, similar to "that is all."

社
社 (shè) can mean "society," "organization," or "agency." It is often used as a suffix in organization names. In older Chinese, it also referred to the god of the land.

~
照相
照相 (zhàoxiàng) and 拍照 (pāizhào) both mean "to take a photo."  照相 is slightly older and more traditional, while 拍照 is more common in modern usage.

~
团体
团体 (tuántǐ) generally refers to a group or organization of people with a shared purpose or interest. It often implies a more formal or structured group than 小组 (xiǎozǔ), which means "small group."

~
应对
应对 (yìngduì) implies dealing with or responding to a situation, often a challenge or problem.  It suggests taking action to address something.  It's more formal than 处理 (chǔlǐ), which also means "to handle" or "deal with."

后
后 (hòu) is a versatile word meaning "back," "behind," "after," "later," and can also refer to an empress or queen.  Pay attention to context to determine the intended meaning.
