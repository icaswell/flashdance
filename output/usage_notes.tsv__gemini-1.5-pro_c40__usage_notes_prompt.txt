~
价钱
价钱 (jiàqian) is a more colloquial term for "price" than 价格 (jiàgé).

~
另
另 (lìng) is used to indicate something different, separate, or additional.  It's often used in constructions like 另外 (lìngwài - in addition, moreover).

~
热水
热水 (rèshuǐ) specifically refers to *hot* water, as opposed to warm or boiling water.

~
哇
哇 (wa) is an onomatopoeia, mimicking the sound of crying, vomiting, or expressing surprise.

~
职务
职务 (zhíwù) encompasses both the position or post held and the duties and responsibilities associated with it.

~
才能
才能 (cáinéng) refers to an innate ability or talent, often suggesting a natural aptitude or gift.

~
大海
大海 (dàhǎi) evokes a more poetic and vast image of the sea or ocean than simply 海 (hǎi).

~
用处
用处 (yòngchu) refers to the usefulness or practicality of something.

~
划分
划分 (huàfēn) means to divide or categorize things according to specific criteria, implying a more systematic or organized division than simply splitting something apart.

~
一同
一同 (yītóng) implies doing something together in the same place and at the same time, emphasizing shared participation in an activity.

~
汽车
汽车 (qìchē) can refer to both cars and buses. Context usually clarifies the specific meaning.

~
不光
不光 (bùguāng) means "not only," introducing additional information or actions beyond the initial statement.  It requires a follow-up clause, usually connected by "而且" (érqiě - but also) or "还" (hái - also).


~
称号
称号 (chēnghào) specifically refers to a title or appellation, often honorary or descriptive, that is bestowed upon someone or something. It carries more weight and formality than simply a name.

~
继承

There are two main verbs for "inherit" in Chinese: 继承 (jìchéng) and 遗传 (yíchuán).  While both relate to passing things down, 继承 refers to inheriting non-physical things like traditions, titles, beliefs, or property.  遗传 is for inheriting physical traits or genetic conditions.  Think of 继承 as "to inherit" and 遗传 as "to be inherited."

~
坏人

While 坏人 literally translates to "bad person," it carries a stronger connotation than simply "bad."  It implies someone who is actively malicious, villainous, or even criminal.  For a more general "bad person," consider using 不好的人 (bù hǎo de rén).

~
救助

救助 carries a more formal and serious tone than other words for "help" like 帮助 (bāngzhù).  It often implies a situation of danger, emergency, or significant hardship.  Think of 救助 as closer to "rescue" or "relief."

~
晓得

晓得 is primarily used in spoken language and has a regional feel, most commonly associated with southern dialects.  知道 (zhīdào) is the more common and versatile word for "to know."

~
调解

调解 emphasizes mediating between two or more parties in a dispute, often with the goal of reaching a compromise.  It is a more formal term than 说和 (shuōhé), which also means "to mediate" but is used in less serious situations.

~
马车

马车 is a general term for horse-drawn carriages.  However, if you want to be more specific, you can use 馬車 (mǎchē) for a general carriage, 馬車 (mǎchē) for a wagon or cart, and 戰車 (zhànchē) for a chariot.

~
码头

While 码头 can refer to any docking area, it more often refers to piers or wharves used for passenger transport.  For cargo docks, 港口 (gǎngkǒu) is a more appropriate term.

~
费用

费用 refers to the cost of a service or activity, such as tuition fees or travel expenses.  It carries a more formal connotation than other words for cost, such as 价格 (jiàgé) for the price of goods or 成本 (chéngběn) for production costs.

~
随后

随后 emphasizes the immediacy of following, suggesting little to no time gap.  It is closer to "immediately after" or "right after."  稍后 (shāo hòu) means "a little later."

~
树叶

树叶 can be both singular and plural, so you wouldn't have to say "树叶们" to pluralize.


~
补助

补助 specifically refers to financial aid or subsidies provided to individuals or organizations, often by the government.  It's different from 津贴 (jīntiē), which are specific allowances given for a particular purpose (like housing allowance).

~
增

增 is often used as a component in compound words, such as 增加 (zēngjiā) "to increase," 增多 (zēngduō) "to increase in number," or 增強 (zēngqiáng) "to strengthen."  It’s less common as a standalone verb.

~
机动车

机动车 is a broad term encompassing all motorized vehicles, including cars, trucks, buses, and motorcycles.  汽车 (qìchē) specifically refers to cars or automobiles.

~
医疗

医疗 refers to the entire system and practice of medical care, including treatment, prevention, and research.  治疗 (zhìliáo) is more specific to the act of treating a disease or injury.

~
或是

或是 implies a choice between two exclusive options, similar to "either... or..."  或者 (huòzhě) can also mean "or," but can also connect more than two options or non-exclusive options.

~
学科

学科 is a more formal and academic term for a field of study than 科目 (kēmù), which usually refers to a school subject.

~
国会

Remember that 国会 corresponds to the legislative body of a specific country.  It's not always a direct equivalent to "parliament" or "congress."  For example, Japan's parliament is called 国会 (guóhuì) in Chinese, while the US Congress is 美国国会 (měiguó guóhuì).

~
镜头

镜头 can refer to both the physical camera lens and a shot or scene in a film.  The context usually clarifies the meaning.

~
间接

间接 means "indirect," emphasizing that something occurs through an intermediary or intermediate step.  It's often used in contrast with 直接 (zhíjiē) "direct."

~
快速

快速 emphasizes speed and efficiency, often implying modern technology or advanced methods.  快 (kuài) is a more general term for "fast."

~
无效

无效 means something is legally or officially not valid or effective.  It carries a more formal tone than 没用 (méiyòng), which simply means "useless" or "ineffective."

~
展现

展现 suggests a gradual or unfolding revelation of something.  It's often used for abstract concepts like talent or potential.  显示 (xiǎnshì) is more general for "to show" or "to display."

~
困惑

困惑 suggests a state of being deeply puzzled or bewildered, often about something complex or difficult to understand.  It is stronger than simply confused and closer to "perplexed."

~
内蒙古

内蒙古 is the shortened form of 内蒙古自治区 (Nèi Měnggǔ Zìzhìqū), the Inner Mongolia Autonomous Region.  Be mindful of the full name in formal contexts.

~
组长

组长 is a general term for a group leader, and the specific responsibilities can vary widely depending on the context.

~
午餐

午餐 is a more formal term for "lunch" than 午饭 (wǔfàn), which is more commonly used in everyday conversation.

~
暴雨

暴雨 indicates heavy rainfall, significantly stronger than just heavy rain.  It suggests potential for flooding or other weather-related damage.

~
塞

塞 has multiple meanings, including "to fill," "to stuff," "a strategic pass," and "Serbia." Pay attention to context to determine the correct meaning.

~
停止

停止 implies a complete cessation of an action or process.  暂停 (zàntíng) means to temporarily pause or suspend something, with the possibility of resuming later.

~
口号

口号 usually refers to slogans used for political, social, or commercial purposes.  标语 (biāoyǔ) can also mean "slogan," but often refers to written slogans or banners.

~
戏曲

戏曲 encompasses a variety of traditional Chinese theatrical forms, including opera and other musical performances.  It is broader than just Chinese opera.


~
急救

急救 refers to providing immediate and temporary medical care in emergency situations.  It is more specific than 救治 (jiùzhì), which refers to medical treatment in general.

~
得了

得了 has two main meanings. One is an imperative meaning "stop it," "cut it out," or "enough!" The other indicates understanding or agreement like “got it,” "okay," or "fine." Tone and context are essential to understanding which meaning is intended.

~
时时

时时, while meaning "often" or "constantly," is somewhat literary or archaic.  常常 (chángcháng), 经常 (jīngcháng), or 总是 (zǒngshì) are more common in modern usage.

~
起码

起码 emphasizes the absolute minimum requirement or lowest acceptable standard.  至少 (zhìshǎo) also means "at least," but doesn't necessarily carry the same sense of a bare minimum.

~
型

型 refers to a general type, model, or style.  类型 (lèixíng) is more specific and refers to a category or classification.

~
海底

海底 specifically refers to the ocean floor or seabed.  水底 (shuǐdǐ) is a more general term for the bottom of any body of water.


~
不大

不大 is generally used before adjectives or verbs and can have different nuances depending on context. It can indicate small size (not big), low frequency (not often), or mild degree (not very).  It can also be a polite way to downplay something.

~
斗争

斗争 carries a more intense and often political connotation than other words for fighting or struggling, such as 奋斗 (fèndòu) "to strive" or  努力 (nǔlì) "to work hard."  It often implies conflict, resistance, or opposition.

~
长大

长大 specifically means "to grow up," referring to the process of maturing from childhood to adulthood.  长大 also carries the connotation of becoming bigger physically.  成长 (chéngzhǎng) is a broader term encompassing various aspects of development, both physical and mental.
