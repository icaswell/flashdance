不一定
This phrase can sometimes function as a polite way to decline an offer or express disagreement. It's softer than a direct "no."

压迫
While the primary meaning relates to political or social oppression, it can also refer to physical pressure, tightness, or a feeling of being stifled.

漏洞
Beyond literal holes, this often refers to flaws in a plan, system, or argument.

~
联络
This word has a stronger implication of ongoing communication or connection than simply contacting someone once.

它们
Remember that inanimate objects and animals generally take the pronoun 它 (tā), while 它们 (tāmen) is specifically for plural inanimate objects and animals.  Don't mix it up with  他们 (tāmen) which is for human plurals.

~
同胞
While it can refer to siblings, it is more commonly used in a broader sense of compatriots or fellow countrymen. It evokes a sense of shared identity and kinship.

图案
This specifically refers to a decorative or artistic pattern, not just any arrangement.

地下室
In modern usage, this term is often associated with Western-style houses and may not be the most appropriate term for traditional Chinese underground structures.

~
不至于
This implies that something negative is unlikely to happen or reach a certain extreme. It's often used to reassure or downplay a concern.

~
晚餐
While 晚餐 is a general term, 晚饭 (wǎnfàn) is a more common and colloquial word for dinner.

水库
This can refer to both natural and artificial reservoirs.

奶茶
In many contexts, especially in cafes, this now usually refers to the Taiwanese style milk tea with tapioca pearls, unless otherwise specified.

订
When referring to making a reservation or booking, 订 is usually followed by a word specifying what is being booked, like 房间 (fángjiān - room), 机票 (jīpiào - air ticket), or 位子 (wèizi - seat).

消费者
This emphasizes the role of someone purchasing goods or services.  客户 (kèhù) is a broader term encompassing anyone with a business relationship with a company, not just buyers.

灯光
This refers specifically to artificial light, often in a stage setting or decorative context.

联想
This word also means "Lenovo", the Chinese multinational technology company.

~
探讨
This suggests a deeper and more academic discussion than just casual conversation.  It often implies exploring a topic systematically.

天文
This can encompass both astronomy and astrology in some contexts.

矮小
This adjective typically describes people, plants, or buildings.  It emphasizes shortness in stature combined with smallness overall.

~
反
This character has a wide range of meanings and uses.  Pay close attention to context to determine the specific meaning.  As a verb, it can sometimes simply mean to “turn over” or “flip”.

~
末
This character has many meanings, some of which are archaic.  In addition to the meanings listed in the input, it is also used as a suffix indicating powder (e.g.  药末 - powdered medicine).

有劲儿
This is mostly used in northern China.  The equivalent expression in southern China is “有力气” (yǒu lìqi).

汇报
This term implies a formal report, often to a superior or a group.  It carries more weight than just telling someone something.

学位
This refers to the qualification itself, not the physical location.  For "place in school", use 学位 (xuéwèi), or even more commonly, 名额 (míng'é).

一向
This refers to a consistent behavior or state over a period of time leading up to the present.  It does not necessarily mean forever.

贫困
Refers to a state of being extremely poor, often with connotations of hardship and lack of basic necessities.

打球

担保
This implies taking responsibility for someone or something, often involving a legal or financial obligation.

仍旧
This emphasizes the continuation of a state or action despite changes or expectations.  It is more formal than 还是 (háishì).

女生
Specifically refers to female students.  For women in general, use 女性 (nǚxìng) or 女的 (nǚde).

球员

外来

用心
This describes putting effort and thought into something, often with an implication of care or dedication.

~
有要事
This phrase is very formal and is more likely to appear in written Chinese. A more common and colloquial way to say this would be “有重要的事.”

~
月底
Be careful not to confuse this with “月末.” 月底 specifically refers to the last day of the month, while 月末 can refer to the last few days of the month.

~
护
When used as a verb, 护 (hù) often implies a sense of protectiveness or defensiveness, similar to "shield" or "guard."  It suggests actively preventing harm or damage.  While it can mean simply "protect," the nuance is stronger than the more general 保护 (bǎohù).  For example, 护送 (hùsòng) means to escort or protect someone on a journey.

~
不一定
This can also function as a one-word reply to a question, meaning “not necessarily” or “maybe.”

~
打破
This word carries a nuance of breaking something that was previously whole or intact, often with some force or impact.  Consider the difference between 打破 (dǎpò) and 弄坏 (nònghuài).  打破 suggests shattering a vase or breaking a record, while 弄坏 implies causing something to malfunction or become unusable, like spoiling food or breaking a toy.

~
压迫
Beyond the political sense of "oppress," 压迫 can also describe physical pressure or constriction, such as feeling oppressed by heat or tight clothing.  It can also describe psychological stress.  The specific meaning depends on the context.

~
漏洞
This can refer to a concrete hole or gap, but more figuratively, it can mean a flaw, weakness, or loophole in a system, plan, or argument.

~
联络
This word implies establishing or maintaining contact, often for a specific purpose, such as coordinating activities or exchanging information. It carries a slightly more formal tone than 联系 (liánxì), which is a more general term for contact.

~
它们
Remember that this pronoun is only used for inanimate objects or animals. For people, use "他们" (tāmen) or "她们" (tāmen).

~
同胞
While literally meaning "born of the same parents," 同胞 is more commonly used to refer to fellow citizens or compatriots, emphasizing a shared national identity.  It can evoke a sense of solidarity or kinship.

~
图案
This refers to a decorative or artistic design, often repeating or geometric, such as the pattern on fabric or wallpaper.  It differs from 模式 (móshì), which refers to a model, pattern, or mode of operation, often in a more technical or abstract sense.

~
地下室
In modern usage, this can refer to any basement or cellar, regardless of whether it's used for storage or other purposes.

~
不至于
This phrase implies that something is unlikely to reach a certain extreme or negative outcome. It expresses a degree of reassurance or relief that a situation is not as bad as feared.

~
晚餐
It's helpful to know the measure words for 晚餐: 份 (fèn) for portions, 顿 (dùn) for occasions, and 次 (cì) for times/instances.

灯光

联想

~

~
矮小
This term describes someone who is both short and small in overall stature, not just short in height.

~
反
This character is extremely versatile and can act as an adverb, adjective, noun, or verb.  Understanding its diverse uses is key to mastering Chinese.
