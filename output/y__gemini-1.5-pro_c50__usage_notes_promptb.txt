---
历史
历史 (lìshǐ) can refer to both "history" (the academic discipline) and "history" (past events).  Be mindful of the context to determine the specific meaning.

---
对方
对方 (duìfāng) is generally used to refer to another party in a more formal or adversarial context. For casual conversations about "the other person," consider using 他/她/他们 (tā/tāmen) or 那个人/那些人 (nàge rén/nàxiē rén).

---
式体验
式体验 (shì tǐyàn) on its own is not a complete word or phrase.  It typically appears as part of a compound, like 沉浸式体验 (chénjìnshì tǐyàn, immersive experience).  "-style experience" might be a better translation than simply "experience."  Pay attention to the preceding modifier to understand the full meaning.

---
听力
While 听力 (tīnglì) primarily means "listening ability," in the context of language learning, it specifically refers to "listening comprehension."

---
牛
牛 (niú) can refer to both "cow" and "ox."  The general term for the bovine family is 牛科 (niúkē).  If you need to be specific, 母牛 (mǔniú) is "cow" and 公牛 (gōngniú) is "bull."  水牛 (shuǐniú) refers to "water buffalo."

---
奖励
奖励 (jiǎnglì) implies a reward given for achievement or good behavior.  It carries a more formal and official tone than simply "giving a gift."

---
音乐家
音乐家 (yīnyuèjiā) can refer to both "musician" and "composer," depending on context.  For a performer, you might use 演奏家 (yǎnzòujiā, instrumentalist) or 歌手 (gēshǒu, singer).

---
约会
约会 (yuēhuì) can range from a casual meeting or appointment to a romantic date.  The context is crucial.  For a romantic date specifically, you could use 浪漫的约会 (làngmàn de yuēhuì).

---
多元
多元 (duōyuán) is often used in contexts related to culture, society, or values, meaning "diverse" or "pluralistic."  It's less common in mathematical or scientific contexts.

---
画家
画家 (huàjiā) generally refers to a painter of artistic works, not someone who paints houses.  For the latter, you would use 油漆工 (yóuqīgōng).

---
齿音
齿音 (chǐyīn) refers to sibilant sounds.  Be aware that the specific sounds included can vary slightly depending on the linguistic context.

---
兄长
兄长 (xiōngzhǎng) is a more formal and respectful term for "older brother."  哥哥 (gēge) is more common in everyday conversation.

---
告诉
告诉 (gàosu) specifically means "to tell" information, not simply "to speak."  For general speaking, use 说 (shuō).

---
系领带
系领带 (jì lǐngdài) specifically means "to tie a necktie."  戴领带 (dài lǐngdài) means "to wear a necktie."

---
贪钱
贪钱 (tānqián) is quite a strong and negative term, implying greed or avarice.  Consider the context before using it, as it can be offensive.

---
台湾
Be mindful of the political sensitivities surrounding the term 台湾 (Táiwān).  Depending on the context, including its relationship with mainland China, different terms and expressions might be more appropriate.

---
体力
体力 (tǐlì) refers to physical strength and stamina, not mental strength.  For mental strength, you could use 毅力 (yìlì) or 精神力量 (jīngshen lìliàng).

---
动词
动词 (dòngcí) refers to "verbs" in the grammatical sense.  Make sure to distinguish it from actions or movements themselves.

---
培育
培育 (péiyù) usually implies careful nurturing and development, often of something young or vulnerable, like plants or children.  It's not a general synonym for "to train" or "to educate."

---
东亚
东亚 (dōngyà) typically refers to "East Asia," encompassing countries like China, Japan, South Korea, and sometimes Mongolia.  Be aware that the specific countries included might vary depending on the context.
