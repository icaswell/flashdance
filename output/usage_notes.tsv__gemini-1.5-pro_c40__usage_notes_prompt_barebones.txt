漩儿
Often used in the reduplicated form 漩儿漩儿(de).  For example, 水面上起了漩儿漩儿(shuǐmiàn shàng qǐ le xuánr xuánr de) - swirls appeared on the surface of the water.

硌
This character is often used to describe the uncomfortable feeling of something hard pressing against your body.  A common usage is 牙硌得慌 (yá gè de huāng) - my tooth is really bothering me (from something stuck in it).  It can also describe emotional discomfort.

~
书呆
This term carries a slightly negative connotation, suggesting someone is overly bookish and lacks social skills.  A more neutral term for "studious person" would be 学霸 (xuébà).

~
概率
Closely related to 几率 (jīlǜ), also meaning "probability."  概率 is more formal and often used in technical contexts.

~
缝纫
While it can refer to sewing in general, 缝纫 is often associated with tailoring or making clothes, rather than simple mending.

~
睡袋
Relates to other camping vocabulary like 帐篷 (zhàngpéng - tent) and 野营 (yěyíng - camping).

~
耳塞
Can be confused with 耳机 (ěrjī - headphones/earphones).  Make sure to clarify whether you're talking about blocking sound or listening to audio.

~
反腐
Short for 反腐败 (fǎnfǔbài).  A common term in current Chinese political discourse.

~
螃蟹
Remember the measure word 只 (zhī) for crabs.  You can say 一只螃蟹 (yī zhī pángxiè) - one crab.

~
语法格
A technical term in linguistics.  Not commonly used in everyday conversation.

~
潲
Often used to describe heavy, driving rain.  A similar word is 瓢泼大雨 (piáopō dàyǔ).

~
腐败
Has both literal (rotten) and figurative (corruption) meanings.  Be aware of the context.

~
剥
Covers a range of peeling actions, from fruits and vegetables to shells and skin.  The specific meaning depends on the object.

~
刺绣
Often used to describe traditional Chinese embroidery, which is known for its intricate designs.

~
蹭饭
Informal and colloquial.  Implies eating at someone else's place without contributing.  Avoid using it in formal situations.

~
加湿器
Useful vocabulary for talking about appliances and indoor air quality.

~
帐篷
Remember the measure words 顶 (dǐng) and 座 (zuò) for tents.

~
随和
A positive personality trait, describing someone who is agreeable and easy to get along with.

~
特产
Often used in the context of travel and souvenirs.  Each region in China has its own 特产.

~
捷克语
Remember that 语 (yǔ) is a common suffix for languages.

~
大白天
Emphasizes that something happened in broad daylight, often implying surprise or disbelief.

~
红楼梦
One of the four classic novels of Chinese literature.  A significant cultural touchstone.

~
密度
A scientific term.  Be aware of its technical usage.

蛤蜊
Measure word is 个 (gè).  Often used in seafood dishes.

章鱼
Measure word is 只 (zhī).

发呆
Describes a mental state of being dazed or lost in thought.  Not necessarily negative.

~
开胃
Can have both literal and figurative meanings.  Be mindful of the context.

~
山丘
Refers to small hills, smaller than 山 (shān - mountain).

~
走廊
Refers to an enclosed passageway, either inside a building or outside connecting parts of a building.

~
刺眼
Often used to describe bright sunlight or artificial light that is too strong.  Can also describe garish colors.
